About the Book

The Psychology of Money is a fascinating exploration of the strange ways people think about money and teaches you how to make better sense of one of life's most important topics.

Through 19 short stories, Morgan Housel reveals how psychology, rather than finance theory, drives successful investing. The book explores the complex relationship between money and human behavior, showing how our emotions, biases, and personal experiences shape our financial decisions.

This isn't a book about getting rich quick. It's about understanding the psychological aspects of money that can help you build long-term wealth and happiness.

About Morgan Housel

Morgan Housel is a partner at The Collaborative Fund and a former columnist at The Motley Fool and The Wall Street Journal. He has won the Best in Business Award from the Society of American Business Editors and Writers.

His work has been featured in major publications and he's known for his ability to explain complex financial concepts in simple, relatable terms.
